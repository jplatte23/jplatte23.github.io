Deep Containment

Engineer & Designer | Unity, C#, Perforce

The Game

SCP-inspired underwater horror experience built in Unity as a 13-week summer project, featuring replayable procedural layouts, tension-driven encounters, and system-focused traversal and interaction mechanics.

Technical Focus

  • Procedural Generation: modular hallway tiles, seed-driven layouts, replayable runs
  • Navigation + AI: A* pathfinding/route validation, patrol/chase behaviors, encounter tuning
  • Stealth + Tension: raycast-based back-peeking/visibility checks, timed attack windows
  • Interaction: item pickup/usage, water-current movement forces, objective flow hooks
  • Tools: Unity (C#), Perforce, physics queries, debug logging/iteration

Technical Summary

Deep Containment demonstrates rapid end-to-end gameplay systems development in Unity, combining procedural level generation, A* pathfinding / solvability checks, and enemy AI archetypes with environmental interaction systems (water currents, item usage, and visibility-based peeking) to deliver a complete multi-level horror experience on a short timeline.

My Contributions

  • Built core gameplay systems including player movement, item pickup/usage, and water-current interactions in Unity using C#, iterating on feel and responsiveness through frequent playtesting.
  • Implemented a procedural hallway generator using modular tiles and a seed validation step to ensure each run produces a playable, solvable layout.
  • Developed A* pathfinding + route checks to prevent dead seeds and support consistent objective flow and encounter pacing across generated levels.
  • Created enemy AI archetypes and a raycast-based back-peeking mechanic, tuning detection and attack cadence to maintain tension while keeping encounters fair.
