The Game

Wielderless is a 1-4 player co-op action roguelite where players are cursed weapons searching for bodies to wield them. Players begin each run in weapon form, crawling through a hostile village, luring compatible hosts, possessing their bodies, and fighting to grow strong enough to defeat the village champion.

The core fantasy is that the player is not the hero holding the weapon. The player is the weapon. Hosts are temporary bodies, and losing one sends the player back into vulnerable weapon form.

My Role

I am building the prototype's player-facing systems, multiplayer flow, possession loop, NPC interaction mechanics, and combat feedback. The current focus is proving the core loop in networked play: find a host, test compatibility, lure or possess them, fight through encounters, then survive when the host is lost.

Core Systems

Weapon Form Movement

Weapon form uses physics impulses to make the player feel like a cursed object forcing itself to move. The weapon builds a glow as it begins moving, then bursts forward with force, sound, and Niagara VFX. The goal is to make weapon form feel vulnerable but alive rather than like a passive downed state.

Physics Niagara Game Feel

Host Compatibility & Scan

NPCs have compatibility values that can be revealed with a scan ability. Compatibility creates the host-hunting loop, encouraging players to search for stronger bodies instead of treating NPCs only as enemies.

NPC Data World UI Player Choice

Lure System

In weapon form, players can lure nearby NPCs toward the weapon. The next iteration is a targeted tether system where players aim at a specific host, attempt to charm them, and then possess them if the lure succeeds.

AI Behavior Targeting

Possession

Players can take control of NPC hosts, switching from vulnerable weapon form into a temporary body. Host possession is designed as the central state change of the game.

State Change Replication

Steam Multiplayer

The prototype supports Steam session hosting and joining through session lists and join codes. Core interactions including scanning, luring, possession, weapon movement, and host combat are functional in multiplayer.
