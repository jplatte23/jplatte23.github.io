The Mine (WIP)

Gameplay Engineer & Designer | Unity, C#, Perforce

The Game

A single-player first-person horror game built in Unity, focused on descending through a dangerous mine shaft while being stalked by a Cartoon Cat (SCP). The experience centers on unstable elevator traversal, escalating tension, environmental hazards, and limited player counterplay.

Technical Focus

  • Gameplay Systems: elevator traversal, movement tuning, player counterplay, encounter logic
  • Enemy Behavior: spider attack pacing, shaft traversal, aggression states, flashbang reactions
  • Procedural Animation/VFX: spider leg IK, raycast-based surface detection, grapple/tentacle effects
  • Game Feel: movement audio, camera control, jumpscare timing, post-processing transitions
  • Tools: Unity, C#, Perforce

Elevator Traversal & Tension System

Engineered a custom elevator traversal system designed to make vertical movement feel heavy, unstable, and dangerous. The system uses asymmetric acceleration, braking, slip behavior, and layered movement audio to create tension during the descent.

  • Asymmetric acceleration and braking for weighty elevator movement
  • Slip behavior to create moments of instability during traversal
  • Layered movement audio to reinforce mechanical strain
  • Tuning focused on tension, readability, and player anticipation
Gameplay Systems Traversal Game Feel Audio Feedback

Spider Encounter System

Built a state-driven spider encounter system that controls shaft traversal, attack pacing, aggression logic, flashbang counterplay, and kill-state handling. The goal was to create pressure while preserving enough readability for the player to react.

  • Lightweight state machine for movement, attacking, and paused behavior
  • Attack pacing and randomized timing to reduce scripted-feeling encounters
  • Flashbang counterplay that temporarily interrupts and repositions the spider
  • Kill-volume gating to prevent unfair instant deaths and overlap edge cases

Procedural Spider IK & Grapple VFX

Developed procedural spider leg IK and grapple/tentacle effects to make creature movement feel more grounded and reactive inside the mine shaft. The systems use raycasts, curved line rendering, and wall attachment logic to create dynamic contact with the environment.

  • Raycast-based surface detection for leg and grapple attachment points
  • Curved line rendering for tentacle/grapple-style visual effects
  • Procedural placement to make creature motion react to the environment
  • Tuning focused on readability during fast-paced encounter moments
