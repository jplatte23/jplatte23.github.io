The Bookstore

Engineer & Designer | Unity, C#, Perforce

The Game

A 15-week, 2-person first-person horror game built in Unity, focused on traversal tension, environmental looping, and interactive exploration. The core hook is a seamless “looping” descent where spaces repeat in ways that feel consistent, but wrong... reinforcing the psychological horror tone.

Technical Focus

  • Gameplay Systems: first-person movement, interaction/pickups, encounter logic
  • Looping Level Tech: seamless environment repetition via triggers + state control
  • World Building: authored environments with performance-aware layout decisions
  • Audio Integration: coordinating external SFX delivery and in-engine implementation

Technical Summary

The Bookstore demonstrates end-to-end gameplay and environment systems development in a first-person horror experience, combining traversal and interaction mechanics with a seamless looping level feature, plus polish and integration work needed to ship a playable build under a tight schedule.

My Contributions

  • Implemented core first-person gameplay systems including movement, interaction/pickups, and encounter scripting in Unity using C# to support puzzle-driven traversal and horror pacing.
  • Collaborated on the looping escalator mechanic and environment transitions, ensuring repeated spaces remain consistent and immersive while preventing softlocks and navigation edge cases.
  • Designed and optimized environments for readability and atmosphere, iterating on layout, lighting, and asset placement to maintain performance while reinforcing the game’s tone.
  • Coordinated with an external sound team to integrate unique audio assets and implement in-engine playback and event timing to strengthen atmosphere and player feedback.
  • Supported testing, polish, and public release on itch.io through iteration on bugs, pacing, and presentation under a tight production schedule.
